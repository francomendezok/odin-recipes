Locro Recipe

This is a national dish from Argentina

The image of locro, a hearty stew and national dish of Argentina, is sure to make your mouth water. In a large bowl or pot, you'll see chunks of tender beef or pork, potatoes, squash, corn, and onions cooked to perfection in a flavorful broth made with garlic, paprika, cumin, and other spices. The bright yellow kernels of corn give the dish its distinctive color and add a sweet, earthy flavor to each spoonful. Served piping hot with a side of crusty bread, locro is the ultimate comfort food that's perfect for chilly days and special occasions.

Here is the list of ingredients

  • 1 pound beef brisket or pork shoulder, cut into small cubes
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 cups beef or vegetable broth
  • 2 ears of fresh corn, cut into 2-inch rounds
  • 1 pound yellow potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1/2 pound butternut squash, peeled and diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh cilantro, for garnish

Steps

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the meat and cook until browned on all sides.
  2. Add the onion and garlic to the pot and saut√© until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Stir in the paprika, cumin, and oregano and cook for 1 minute.
  4. Pour in the water and broth and bring to a boil.
  5. Add the corn, potatoes, squash, and red bell pepper to the pot.
  6. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and simmer for 1 1/2 to 2 hours, stirring occasionally, until the meat is tender and the vegetables are cooked through.
  7. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  8. Serve the locro hot, garnished with chopped cilantro.

Enjoy your delicious and comforting bowl of Argentinean locro!
